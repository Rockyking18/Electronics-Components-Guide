What are Electronics?

Electronics is a branch of physics that focuses on the emission, behavior, and effects of electrons and their use in devices. It forms the backbone of modern technology — from smartphones to spacecraft.

At its core, every electronic system is built from a handful of fundamental components, each with a precise function that contributes to the whole.
